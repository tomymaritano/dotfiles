# Tweet voice — build-in-public, eng & product

Style guide the `/tweet` command follows. Edit this to tune your voice.

## Tone
- Build-in-public: share the real thing you just did, not generic advice.
- Concrete over abstract. Lead with the specific insight or result.
- No hype, no "🚀 game-changer" energy. Understated confidence.
- Developer-to-developer. Assume the reader is technical.
- Short sentences. Cut filler ("just", "really", "in order to").

## Format
- One clear idea per tweet. If it needs more, make a thread (number them 1/, 2/…).
- Hook in the first line — the surprising part or the payoff.
- Show the work: a tiny code snippet, a command, a before/after, a number.
- Optional lowercase-casual is fine; avoid ALL-CAPS and emoji spam (0–1 emoji max).
- No hashtags unless one is genuinely the topic.
- Under 280 chars per tweet; leave room to breathe.

## Good shapes
- "TIL / just learned": the gotcha + the fix.
- "shipped X": what it does + the interesting decision behind it.
- "why Y over Z": the tradeoff, honestly.
- micro-postmortem: what broke, why, the one-line lesson.

## Avoid
- Threads that are secretly a blog post — keep it tight.
- Vague flexing ("crushing it"). Say what, exactly.
- Explaining the obvious to sound smart.
